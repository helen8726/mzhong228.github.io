Overview

What is PG?

Project Gutenberg is the oldest digital online library which was found by Michael S. Hart in 1971. Until October 2015, Project Gutenberg has reached 50,000 items in its collection. It is a huge collection of full-text books from public domain including multiple types of digital formats. All of the digitalization and achievements are as a result of volunteer effort.

What are the CHALLENGES?

To redesign the website of PG was a self-initiated project from May 2017 thru July 2017. This redesign tries to focus on those issues:

  1. How to transfer people’s opinion viewing public domain books as old and free stuff to something cool and new?
  2. How to connect the readers together to share their intimate feelings and stories about those literal heritage?
  3. How to make a robust but light weight navigation system for them to get inspired and surprised?
  4. How to make an awesome reading experience on screen but at the same time keep the dignity of sophisticated typography?

What is the reason for me to do it?

The spirit of PG is to make these unfound cultural treasure as free as possible, in long-lasting, open formats that can be used on any digital device. As a computer science background designer I appreciate those open source spirit behind PG, however there were multiple design problems existing in the previous website that made me eager to solve as a design challenge for myself.

What are the improvements?

Through the whole design process, I was trying to focus on solving those design problems:

    Design Problems
  1. Polyhierarchy of information archietecture
  2. The lack of personal profile system
  3. Uncomfortable typography

With too many classification options and too many dimensions, users were required to spend more time to search a book. Additionally, there was no personal profile design in the previous website. Users were unavailable to comment, review, forward and save their favorite books due to the lack of this feature. At same time, unintentional leading and too broad line width decreased the legibility and readability.

I’ll showcase some of the improvements in my design as compared to the previous website.

Case study 1: improving the information archietecture

In order to build a robust but light weight information architecture, I created a new classification for book collections based on genre and popularity. Also I redesigned the menu and searching bar combined with icons and query samples.

Design Process

Since this is a self-initiated project, I followed user-centered design throughout the design process. This includes ideation, rapid prototyping, usabilitiy testing, iteration and all of them after a thourough evaluation of the previous website.

Evaluation & Ideation

In order to get a better sense of our user and to identify the existing usability problems in the previous website, I took user journey mapping and user's flow chart as the main ideation methods.

Persona and User Journey map
User's flow chart when searching a book

From the information I researched, there were couple of design issues which were existing in the previous websites.

  1. Unclear Searching system. Users were confused by so much classifications and options.
  2. Confusing Navigation system. Users have to go back to the homepage again and again.
  3. No Rating system and Personal Profile system. Hard for them to share there opinions and stories.
  4. Terrible typographic design. Uncomfortable reading experience on screen.

Prototype and User testing

To solve the design problems that I found during the evaluation process, I took Rapid Iterative Testing & Evaluation(RITE) as the usability inspection method to identify and remove problems early in the design process before costly prototypes were built. This method required multiple prototypes as soon as the problems were identified.
